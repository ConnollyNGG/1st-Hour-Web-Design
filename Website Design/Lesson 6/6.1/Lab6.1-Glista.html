The Sydney Opera House

The Sydney Opera House is one of the most recognizable attractions in Australia. It was opened by Queen Elizabeth II on October 20, 1973. Over 2 million visitors each year attend various performances, including plays, symphonies, and ballets to name a few. In addition to be a center for entertainment, it also hosts a number of meetings, including the 2007 AOECm which was attended by the 21 Asia Pacific states!

The opera house includes:

  • A concert hall that seats 2,679.
  • An opera theatre that seats 1,547.
  • A drama theatre that seats 544.
  • A playhouse theatre that seats 398.
  • A studio that can accommodate up to 364 people.
  • A reception hall which includes 4 restaurants.
